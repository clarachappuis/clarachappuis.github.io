Introduction

Just finished an amazing movie and unsure what to watch next? Don’t worry we’ve got you covered! Every movie you watch has a unique signature, a blend of themes and ideas beyond simple genres. What if the key to finding your next favorite film is not in the usual categories like "action" or "romance," but in uncovering deeper, hidden topics that truly reflect the movie? In this analysis, we dive into the art of movie recommendation. Using Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA), a tool for discovering hidden topics in movies’ plots, we go beyond surface-level genres to identify the intricate themes that define each film. These topics form the foundation of a content-based recommendation system, helping us find movies with similar thematics.

The Genres That Steal the Spotlight in the Movie Industry

Let’s roll the reel and uncover which genres are the true stars of the show and maybe stumble upon a few hidden gems along the way!
First, by analyzing the CMU Movie dataset, we see that we have 133 different genres in the database. Let's find the most recurrent ones:

Drama takes the lead as the most popular genre (what can we say, people love a bit of drama!). Following closely are thriller, comedy, action, and romance, rounding out the top five. This shows that people enjoy excitement and real-life stories. On the other end, niche genres like 'Cult' and 'Political drama' are much less common, which isn't surprising given their more specific audience.

To discover the underlying topics of the movies with LDA we will need the synopses. We decide not to use the movie summaries since they encode significantly less context and would result in less confident clustering. The synopses can be found in the MPST dataset.

In addition to synopses, we also need movie ratings for our analysis. We will explore whether movies with similar themes tend to have similar ratings and examine if ratings are essential for our recommendation system. Ratings provide valuable insights in the context of recommendations, as they directly indicate how much viewers enjoyed a movie. To gather this data, we found an IMDb dataset online that includes movie ratings.
